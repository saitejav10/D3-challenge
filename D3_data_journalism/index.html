Correlations Discovered Between lack of healthcare and poverty

The visualization above displays a positive correlation between poverty and lack of healthcare. There is a visible trend that suggests that the states with high percentage of poverty tends to lack healthcare than the states with little to no poverty. Mississippi and New Mexico ranks highest in terms of Poverty while Texas and Georgia rank highest in terms of lack of healthcare. The overall trend is not set in stone as there are some very clear outliers withing the chart. For example, there are states such as New Hampshiere with relatively low poverty but have high percentage of lack of healthcare compared to states such as Kentucky and Washignton D.C who have very high rate of poverty.
